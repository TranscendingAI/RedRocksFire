## 14. Voice / Tone Mini-Guide

When drafting new copy, lean on these rules:

1. **Lead with the *client's* stakes**, not with what RRFPS does.
   > *Bad:* "RRFPS provides fire alarm service."
   > *Better:* "When your fire alarm goes offline, every minute matters.
   > We pick up the phone."

2. **Numbers > adjectives.** "90-minute response" beats "fast response."

3. **Active voice, present tense.** "We respond within 90 minutes," not
   "our team will be dispatched promptly."

4. **No vendor-speak.** Avoid: *solutions, leveraged, robust, world-class,
   best-in-class, state-of-the-art.* Use: *protection, partner, advocate,
   accountable, dependable, comprehensive.*

5. **Use family once. Then earn it.** The "One Family" pillar is
   meaningful — overuse cheapens it. Save it for About + CEO message.

6. **Punchy headlines, **period at the end.** Brand strategy uses
   sentence-fragment headlines with terminal periods ("Your Partner in
   Life Safety, Not Just Your Vendor."). Honor that cadence.

7. **Never lead with fear-mongering ("fire kills")**. The brand voice is
   *protective and confident*, not alarming.
